Customer journey

What the passenger experiences

The passenger sees one simple service: order, track, invoice, complain and rate RK response. Behind the scenes, the ecosystem does the heavy lifting.

1. Scan QR / open link

Passenger enters name, phone, train, coach and berth.

2. Choose menu

Passenger selects Pantry menu, Base Kitchen menu or RK Partner menu.

3. Track status

Accepted, preparing, out-for-delivery, OTP and delivered become visible.

4. Raise concern

Feedback or complaint creates a ticket visible to command and care teams.

Customer app is simple because the operating complexity is behind it05

Exception design

What happens when any point says No?

A strong system is not only the happy path. It should show management exactly how failure is controlled.

No stock in pantry

Rake manager can offer alternate pantry item or cancel with customer notice. Base Kitchen and Partner menus remain separate paths.

Base kitchen direct issue

Base Kitchen handles partial quantity, delay, ready status, invoice and dispute directly in its app.

Rider delay or COD issue

Rider status is visible; rake manager and customer care can intervene before complaint becomes serious.

Catalog rejected

Control Tower rejects with note. Item stays hidden from passenger app until corrected.

Partner direct issue

RK Partner App handles partner order availability and fulfilment status; unresolved issues go to care/central visibility.

Management escalation

Central Command uses risk dashboard for train, vendor, rider and route-wise corrective action.

No path is hidden; every failure becomes a controlled workflow11
