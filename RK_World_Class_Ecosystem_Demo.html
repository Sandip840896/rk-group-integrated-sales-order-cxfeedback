Customer journey

What the passenger experiences

The passenger sees one simple Meals On Wheels service: branded opening screen, QR journey context, passenger details, menu selection, cart review, COD order, tracking, FAQ, complaint and rating.

1. Train QR opens context

QR link can carry train, rake, Rack Manager and rider details into the Customer App.

2. Passenger and menu filters

Passenger saves train/seat details, then filters by source, meal slot and food preference.

3. Cart and COD pilot

Customer reviews quantity, total payable and COD mode before confirming order.

4. Track, FAQ and complaint proof

Tracking by phone, FAQ support, feedback ticket and proof image/audio/video upload are available.

Exception design

What happens when any point says No?

A strong system is not only the happy path. It should show management exactly how failure is controlled.

No stock in pantry

Rack Manager can offer alternate pantry item or cancel with customer notice. Base Kitchen and Partner menus remain separate paths.

Base kitchen direct issue

Base Kitchen handles partial quantity, delay, ready status, invoice PDF and dispute settlement directly in its app.

Rider delay or COD issue

Rider status is visible; rake manager and customer care can intervene before complaint becomes serious.

Catalog rejected

Control Tower rejects with note. Item stays hidden from passenger app until corrected.

Partner direct issue

RK Partner App handles partner order availability and fulfilment status; unresolved issues go to care/central visibility.

Admin / access issue

Admin Console can disable IDs, adjust app rights and restrict staff by role, scope and permission level.

No path is hidden; every failure becomes a controlled workflow12
